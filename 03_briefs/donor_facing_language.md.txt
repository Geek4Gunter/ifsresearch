# Donor-Facing Language: IFS Research Portfolio

This document provides language suitable for donor conversations, proposals, and stewardship communications.

---

## Core Framing

Families are the primary social institution through which children receive stability, care, and formation. When family structures weaken, downstream consequences emerge across education, economic mobility, mental health, and civic life.

IFS exists to study these dynamics carefully and communicate findings responsibly.

---

## What Makes the Work Distinctive

IFS research does not begin with policy prescriptions or ideological commitments. It begins with empirical questions:

- What patterns are observable?
- What mechanisms explain them?
- What interventions reduce risk without creating new harm?

This posture allows the organization to engage complex issues with credibility and restraint.

---

## Areas of Impact

- **Family Formation:** Clarifying how commitment, timing, and stability affect long-term outcomes.
- **Parents and Work:** Evaluating whether family-support policies function as intended in real-world conditions.
- **Technology and Children:** Identifying exposure pathways and incentive structures that shape childhood.
- **Place-Based Renewal:** Understanding how families experience different institutional ecosystems.

---

## Why Philanthropic Support Matters

High-quality research is necessary but insufficient. The work requires:
- Time for synthesis and integration
- Careful translation for public audiences
- Protection from reactive or politicized framing

Philanthropic partnership enables IFS to pursue long-term understanding rather than short-term attention.
