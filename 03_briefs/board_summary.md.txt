# Board-Level Research Summary: IFS Portfolio

**Purpose:**  
To provide a concise, high-level overview of the Institute for Family Studies’ research portfolio, intellectual posture, and strategic value.

This summary is designed for board-level orientation and governance conversations.

---

## 1. Core Thesis

IFS research is grounded in a consistent thesis:

**Family structure and stability are foundational social institutions that shape the well-being of children, adults, and communities in measurable ways.**

The organization’s work focuses on identifying upstream variables, clarifying mechanisms, and evaluating practical levers that influence family outcomes.

---

## 2. Major Areas of Work

### Family Formation and Stability
Research on marriage, cohabitation, and commitment examines how timing, intention, and sequence affect long-term relationship durability and child well-being. The emphasis is on mechanisms rather than moral assertion.

### Work, Economics, and Families
Policy-oriented research evaluates how economic pressures and institutional design shape family life, with particular attention to paid leave, workplace norms, and implementation realities.
